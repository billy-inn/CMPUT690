Júlio César Jacobi (born 2 September 1986), known as Júlio César, is a Brazilian footballer who plays for S.L. Benfica in the Portuguese first division, as a goalkeeper.
Born in Guaramirim, Santa Catarina, Júlio César started his professional career at Botafogo de Futebol e Regatas, signing a 5-year contract in August 2005. He made his first division debut in a 0–0 home draw against Sport Club Corinthians Paulista, on 26 November 2006.
In January 2008, Júlio César moved to Portugal, signing with C.F. Os Belenenses. He first played in the league on 23 February, against C.S. Marítimo, replacing striker Weldon in the 29th minute after veteran goalkeeper Paulo Costinha had been sent off, in an eventual 1–3 home loss.
After having played all the matches and minutes in his first full season – Belenenses was relegated, but later reinstated – he signed with Lisbon neighbours Sport Lisboa e Benfica, thus reuniting with former manager Jorge Jesus. In his debut season, he backed up Quim, demoting José Moreira to third-choice, and starting in the club's UEFA Europa League campaign.
